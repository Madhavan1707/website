About Me

Building the systems that keep vehicles connected and people safe.

I'm a software engineer at Visteon Corporation in Pune, working at the intersection of Android framework development and safety-critical automotive systems. My day-to-day involves writing the embedded Android layer that makes real vehicles communicate — with cloud backends, emergency services, and fleet systems — in real time.

I've built the emergency call stack (eCall, bCall, SOS) that handles EU regulatory compliance, designed IPC architectures that cut latency by 30%, and shipped vehicle tracking systems running sub-50ms end-to-end. Before automotive, I was a Data Engineer at AB InBev GCC in Bengaluru, building PySpark pipelines on AWS that processed 5+ TB a day.

I studied Computer Science at VIT Vellore — where I picked up Android and never really stopped.
